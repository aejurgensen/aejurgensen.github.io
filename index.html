Assignment 11

My blocks for black and white hat visualizations, and my remade "misleading viz from the media."

  • My black-hat visualization using mushroom data from the UCI ML Data Reposirot
    • Two variables (odor and edibility) are combined and displayed along the x-axis, making the tick labels impossible to read, and the data confusing. The two variables should have been encoded separately for clarity (e.g. only one on the x-axis and the other as color).
    • The bars don't occur in any sensical order.
    • The bars are all filled with yellow, a difficult color to distinguish on a white background since there is no distinct stroke around the bars.
    • There are no annotations or tooltips to indicate the actual values of the bars, nor are there any horizontal lines in the background to help guide the user's eye as they try to "read" the values of the bars.
    • The x-axis is not labeled and the categories are not obvious.
    • The source of the data is not provided.
  • My white-hat visualization using the same mushroom data. This visualziation corrects the problems of the black-hat version:
    • Only a single variable (odor) is displayed along the x-axis and the second variable (edibility) is encoded separately as color.
    • Annotations with values are provided above each bar.
    • The odor groups are ordered from the one with the tallest bar to the one with the shortest.
    • The y-axis ticks begin at 0.
    • The y-axis is labeled.
    • A legend is provided for color.
    • The color red associated with "poisonous," the dangerous category.
    • The source of the data is provided.
  • My improved version of the following misleading chart displayed by Fox News, captured by MediaMatters.org, and discussed in an article by Business Insider (which is where I found it).
    • Most importantly, I started the y-axis of the bart chart from 0! The original version made the changes between the quarters look more drastic than they really were.
    • I remade the 3-D bars as 2-D. The 3-D rendering of the bars gave them the appearance of being taller (and the amounts they represented being greater) than was actually the case.
    • I removed the nonsensical horizontal lines that weren't associated with any of the values of the y-axis tick marks, and opted instead to add annotations with the actual values above each of the bars.
    • I changed the title from "Federal Welfare Received in US," which sounds like it should be an amount in dollars, to "US Welfare Recipients," which better communicates that the chart is displaying numbers of people.
    • I removed "M" from the y-axis tick labels and added instead "(millions)" to the y-axis label itself so that the tick labels are shorter.
    • I simplified the y-axis label from "Number of Americans" to "Citizens."
    • I changed x-axis tick labels to a shorter abbreviated form that is common in business so they didn't need to wrap onto a second line (though the originals probably weren't misleading, they were annoying).
    • I provided my own source for the data, and did so in a way that communicates the uncertainty about the data itself. (Note that the US Census Survey is only conducted every ten years, so it wouldn't have had quarterly data for 2009, 2010, and 2011. The US Census Bureau, however, conducts many different surveys on a variety of schedules, so perhaps Fox News meant the USCS and just didn't specify which survey the data was actually from?)
